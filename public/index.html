CHAPTER 01 — FOUNDATIONS
What Are Options?
A complete beginner's guide to options contracts, rights, obligations, and how premiums work.

An option is a financial contract that gives the buyer the right — but not the obligation — to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specific price (the strike price) on or before a specific date (the expiry date).

Two Types of Options

CALL Option (CE): The right to BUY at the strike price. Buy a CALL when you expect prices to RISE.

PUT Option (PE): The right to SELL at the strike price. Buy a PUT when you expect prices to FALL.

Real World Example

Nifty is at 22,500. You buy a 22,500 CE for ₹142 (lot 25 = ₹3,550 total).

If Nifty rises to 22,800: Your Call is worth ₹300. Profit = (300−142)×25 = ₹3,950 (+111%)

If Nifty stays at 22,400: Your Call expires worthless. Loss = ₹3,550 (100% of premium)

⚠ Critical Warning

Options are wasting assets. Every day, Theta erodes the value. OTM options expire worthless ~80% of the time. Never risk more than you can afford to lose entirely.

Quiz: Nifty is at 22,800. You hold a 22,500 CE. What is the minimum intrinsic value of your Call?
A) ₹300 per unit (₹7,500 per lot)
B) ₹0 — it's OTM
C) ₹142 — the premium paid
CHAPTER 02
Nifty Mechanics
Understanding NIFTY Options

NIFTY 50 options trade on NSE. Each lot = 25 units. Weekly expiry every Thursday 15:30 IST. European style — cash settled. ATM strike = nearest 50-point interval to current spot.

Key Numbers

Lot Size: 25 · Tick Size: ₹0.05 · Settlement: Cash · Max Pain: computed from total OI across all strikes

CHAPTER 04
Premium Decomposition

Premium = Intrinsic Value + Time Value + Volatility Risk Premium. Time value and VRP bleed away daily via Theta — even if Nifty doesn't move.

Smart Buyer Rule

Only buy options when IV is low (VIX below 14). Never buy options when VIX spikes — you're paying peak insurance prices.

CHAPTER 06
Gamma — The Acceleration Greek

Gamma (Γ) measures how fast Delta changes. ATM options near expiry have very high Gamma — small moves cause explosive premium changes on expiry day.

GEX — Gamma Exposure

When market makers are short Gamma, they amplify moves. When long Gamma, they dampen moves and create range-bound markets.

⚠ The Weekend Trap

You pay 3 days of Theta over the weekend. ATM option losing ₹20/day = ₹60 loss before market opens Monday.

CHAPTER 08
Vega — Trading Volatility Itself

Vega (ν) measures how much an option's price changes for a 1% change in Implied Volatility.

Buy options when VIX is LOW (below 14). Sell options when VIX is HIGH (above 20). After big events, IV crush can wipe profits even when direction is correct.

CHAPTER 11

PCR = Total Put OI ÷ Total Call OI. Use as a contrarian indicator.

CHAPTER 12
Max Pain Theory

Max Pain is the strike where the maximum number of options expire worthless — causing maximum pain for option buyers. Market makers are incentivized to keep price near Max Pain at expiry.

Max Pain + PCR + OI Walls = Triple Confirmation

When all three align at the same strike, expiry pinning probability is very high.

CHAPTER 13
OI + Greeks Combined Analysis

1. Find Max Pain level and highest OI strikes.
2. Check Delta positioning — is smart money long or short Delta?
3. Check GEX — are market makers short Gamma (amplify moves) or long Gamma (dampen)?
4. Check Theta positioning.
5. Combine with FII net futures for final directional bias.

CHAPTER 14
Implied Volatility — The Fear Gauge

Implied Volatility (IV) is the market's forward-looking expectation of price movement embedded in option premiums.

The VIX Trade Rule

India VIX below 11 = no trade. 11–14 = next week expiry only. 14–18 = normal trading. 18–25 = best opportunities. Above 25 = defined risk structures only.

CHAPTER 15
IV Skew & Volatility Surface

OTM puts almost always have higher IV than OTM calls. Institutions constantly buy OTM puts as tail risk hedges. When put skew is very steep, sell put spreads or buy call spreads.

CHAPTER 16
Iron Condor Mastery

The Iron Condor is a 4-leg neutral strategy. Buy 1 OTM Put (lower) + Sell 1 OTM Put + Sell 1 OTM Call + Buy 1 OTM Call (upper). Net credit = max profit. Ideal: VIX above 18, stock in a clear range.

CHAPTER 17
Spreads & Calendar Spreads

Bull Call Spread: Buy ATM Call + Sell OTM Call. Net debit. Profit if underlying rises.
Calendar Spread: Buy far-dated + Sell near-dated at same strike. Near-dated decays faster. Best in low-IV environments.

CHAPTER 18
Expiry Week Playbook

Monday: Stop hunt. Avoid. Watch only.
Tuesday: Real bias emerges. Wait for CHoCH.
Wednesday: Best risk/reward entry day.
Thursday Expiry: Exit all profitable positions by 2 PM. No new entries after noon.

⚠ Expiry Day Rule

No new entries after 2:00 PM on expiry day. This is where most retail accounts blow up.

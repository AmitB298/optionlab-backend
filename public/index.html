CHAPTER 01 — FOUNDATIONS
What Are Options?
A complete beginner's guide to options contracts, rights, obligations, and how premiums work.
ऑप्शन कॉन्ट्रैक्ट, अधिकार, दायित्व और प्रीमियम की पूरी शुरुआती गाइड।

An option is a financial contract that gives the buyer the right — but not the obligation — to buy or sell an underlying asset at a specific price (called the strike price) on or before a specific date (the expiry date).

Two Types of Options

CALL Option (CE): The right to BUY the underlying at the strike price. You buy a CALL when you expect prices to RISE.

PUT Option (PE): The right to SELL the underlying at the strike price. You buy a PUT when you expect prices to FALL.

Real World Example

Nifty is at 22,500. You buy a 22,500 CE (Call) expiring Thursday for ₹142 premium (lot size 25). Total cost = ₹3,550.

If Nifty rises to 22,800 by expiry: Your Call is worth at least ₹300 intrinsic value. Profit = (300 − 142) × 25 = ₹3,950 profit (111%)

If Nifty stays at 22,400: Your Call expires worthless. Loss = ₹3,550 (100% of premium)

Premium Components
ComponentDescriptionDepends On
Intrinsic ValueReal value if exercised todaySpot vs Strike
Time ValueHope + uncertainty premiumDTE, Volatility
ExtrinsicIV crush riskVIX, events
⚠ Critical Warning

Options are wasting assets. Every day you hold a long option, time decay (Theta) erodes its value — even if the underlying doesn't move. OTM options expire worthless ~80% of the time. Never risk more than you can afford to lose entirely.

एक ऑप्शन एक वित्तीय अनुबंध है जो खरीदार को अधिकार देता है — लेकिन दायित्व नहीं — किसी निश्चित मूल्य पर (जिसे स्ट्राइक प्राइस कहते हैं) एक निश्चित तिथि से पहले (एक्सपायरी तिथि) खरीदने या बेचने का।

दो प्रकार के ऑप्शन

CALL ऑप्शन (CE): स्ट्राइक प्राइस पर खरीदने का अधिकार। जब आप कीमत बढ़ने की उम्मीद करते हैं तो CALL खरीदते हैं।

PUT ऑप्शन (PE): स्ट्राइक प्राइस पर बेचने का अधिकार। जब आप कीमत गिरने की उम्मीद करते हैं तो PUT खरीदते हैं।

CHAPTER 05 — THE GREEKS
Delta — The Speed of Your Option
How Delta tells you how much your option moves for every ₹1 move in Nifty.
Delta बताता है कि Nifty में ₹1 की चाल से आपका ऑप्शन कितना हिलेगा।

Delta (Δ) measures how much an option's price changes for a ₹1 move in the underlying. It's the most important Greek for directional traders.

Delta Ranges
Option TypeDelta RangeMeaning
Deep ITM Call0.8 – 1.0Moves almost like the underlying
ATM Call~0.550 paise gain per ₹1 Nifty move
OTM Call0.1 – 0.4Small move for same Nifty change
Deep ITM Put-0.8 to -1.0Gains strongly as Nifty falls
ATM Put~-0.550 paise gain per ₹1 Nifty fall
Delta as Probability Proxy

A Delta of 0.30 on a Call option roughly means 30% probability that option expires in-the-money. Deep OTM options have low deltas AND low probability of profit.

Delta Hedging — What Institutions Do

Market makers use delta hedging to stay directionally neutral. If they sell 1,000 ATM Calls (delta 0.5), they buy 500 Nifty futures to hedge. This creates GEX (Gamma Exposure) — the reason Nifty pins near certain strikes near expiry.

Delta (Δ) मापता है कि अंतर्निहित में ₹1 की चाल से ऑप्शन की कीमत कितनी बदलती है।

Delta की सीमाएं

Deep ITM Call: Delta 0.8–1.0 (underlying जैसे चलता है)
ATM Call: Delta ~0.5 (Nifty में ₹1 = ऑप्शन में 50 पैसे)
OTM Call: Delta 0.1–0.4 (कम असर)

CHAPTER 02
Nifty Mechanics
Understanding NIFTY Options

NIFTY 50 options trade on NSE. Each lot = 25 units. Weekly expiry every Thursday 15:30 IST. ATM strike is nearest 50-point interval to current spot.

Key Numbers

Lot Size: 25 | Tick Size: ₹0.05 | Settlement: Cash (no physical delivery) | Max Pain: computed from total OI across all strikes

NIFTY options follow European style — they can only be exercised at expiry, not before. This means no early assignment risk unlike American-style options.

⚠ OTM Buyer Warning

Buying deep OTM options requires a large, fast move in your direction. They expire worthless ~85% of the time. ATM is the sweet spot for directional trades.

CHAPTER 04
Premium Decomposition

Every option premium = Intrinsic Value + Time Value + Volatility Risk Premium.

What You're Paying For

When you pay ₹142 for a Nifty ATM Call: ~₹0 intrinsic (ATM) + ~₹85 time value + ~₹57 volatility risk premium. As expiry approaches, the ₹85 + ₹57 bleed away via Theta — even if Nifty doesn't move.

Smart Buyer Rule

Only buy options when IV is low (VIX below 14). You pay cheap premium and benefit if volatility expands. Never buy options when VIX spikes — you're paying peak insurance prices.

CHAPTER 06
Gamma — The Acceleration Greek

Gamma (Γ) measures how fast Delta changes. ATM options near expiry have very high Gamma — a small Nifty move causes a large Delta change, which means explosive premium moves on expiry day.

Gamma Risk on Expiry Day

Thursday expiry: ATM options can move 5–10× their value in a single 15-minute candle. This is why expiry is both the highest opportunity AND highest risk day. Gamma squeezes cause rapid directional moves as market makers re-hedge.

GEX — Gamma Exposure

When market makers are short Gamma (sold ATM options), they must buy the underlying as it rises and sell as it falls — amplifying moves. When long Gamma, they do the opposite — dampening moves and creating range-bound markets.

CHAPTER 07
Theta — Time Decay is Your Enemy

Theta (Θ) is the amount of premium your option loses each day just from time passing — even if Nifty doesn't move at all.

⚠ The Weekend Trap

If you hold options over the weekend, you pay 3 days of Theta in the Monday open. A Nifty ATM option losing ₹20/day over weekend = ₹60 loss before market even opens. Always account for weekend theta in your entry timing.

CHAPTER 08
Vega — Trading Volatility Itself

Vega (ν) measures how much an option's price changes for a 1% change in Implied Volatility. It's the Greek that makes options buying most profitable during sudden fear spikes.

Vega Strategy

Buy options when VIX is LOW (below 14) — Vega is cheap. If VIX expands to 18+, your long Vega position profits from IV expansion PLUS any directional move.

Sell options when VIX is HIGH (above 20) — Collect inflated Vega premium, profit as fear subsides and IV contracts back to mean.

IV Crush Warning

After big events (Budget, RBI policy, Elections), VIX often collapses 30–50% in a single session. Even if Nifty moves in your direction, IV crush can result in a losing long option position. Always check IV rank before buying ahead of events.

CHAPTER 09

No Greek works in isolation. Understanding how Delta, Gamma, Theta, and Vega interact determines whether your trade is properly structured.

The Trifecta Trade

Best directional buy: ATM option, VIX below 14, 3–7 DTE. You get maximum Delta sensitivity, high Gamma for explosive moves, low Vega so IV expansion only helps you, and enough DTE to avoid extreme Theta erosion on day 1.

CHAPTER 10
Open Interest — The Institutional Fingerprint

Open Interest (OI) is the total number of outstanding option contracts that have NOT been settled. Unlike volume, OI shows you net institutional positioning — not just activity.

OI Change Interpretation
PriceOI ChangeInterpretation
RisingRisingLONG BUILDUP — Bullish fresh positions
RisingFallingSHORT COVERING — Bears exiting, bullish
FallingRisingSHORT BUILDUP — Bearish fresh positions
FallingFallingLONG UNWINDING — Bulls exiting, bearish
OI Walls

Strike with highest Call OI = resistance wall (market makers short calls defend this ceiling). Strike with highest Put OI = support floor. Price often pins between these walls as market makers delta hedge.

CHAPTER 11
Put-Call Ratio (PCR)

PCR = Total Put OI ÷ Total Call OI. It measures the balance of bearish vs bullish positioning in the options market.

PCR Interpretation
PCR ValueInterpretationStrategy
> 1.5Extremely bearish sentimentContrarian BULLISH signal
1.2 – 1.5Moderately bearish → bullishLook for CALL entries
0.7 – 1.2Neutral / balancedRange-bound strategies
< 0.7Extreme complacencyContrarian BEARISH signal
⚠ PCR is Contrarian

High PCR means too many bears — market often squeezes them UP. Low PCR means too many bulls — market often reverses DOWN. Use PCR as a contrarian sentiment indicator, not a directional predictor.

CHAPTER 12
Max Pain Theory

Max Pain is the strike price at which the maximum number of options contracts (both calls and puts) expire worthless — causing the maximum pain for option buyers.

Market makers and large institutions who are net short options are incentivized to keep price near Max Pain at expiry — minimizing their payout obligations.

How to Use Max Pain

On expiry day, if spot is far from Max Pain, there is gravitational pull back toward it. The last 90 minutes of trading (1:30 PM – 3:30 PM) often shows this pinning behavior strongly.

Max Pain + PCR + OI Walls = Triple Confirmation

When all three align — Max Pain level, PCR extreme reading, and large OI wall at same strike — the probability of price pinning there at expiry is very high.

CHAPTER 13
OI + Greeks Combined Analysis

The highest-probability trades come from combining OI analysis with Greeks positioning. This is what separates institutional traders from retail.

The Setup Framework

1. Find the Max Pain level and highest OI strikes (call wall + put wall).
2. Check Delta positioning — is smart money long or short Delta?
3. Check Gamma exposure — are market makers short Gamma (amplify moves) or long Gamma (dampen)?
4. Check Theta positioning — option sellers want range, buyers want a move.
5. Combine with FII net futures for final directional bias.

CHAPTER 14
Implied Volatility — The Fear Gauge

Implied Volatility (IV) is the market's forward-looking expectation of price movement embedded in option premiums. It's derived backward from option prices using Black-Scholes.

IV vs Historical Volatility

When IV > HV: Options are expensive. Sellers have edge. Premium is overpriced relative to actual realized moves.
When IV < HV: Options are cheap. Buyers have edge. Market is underestimating upcoming moves.

CHAPTER 15
IV Skew & Volatility Surface

IV Skew refers to the fact that different strikes have different implied volatilities — even for the same expiry. OTM puts almost always have higher IV than OTM calls in equity markets.

Why Skew Exists

Institutional portfolio managers constantly buy OTM puts as tail risk hedges. This excess demand drives up OTM put IV relative to calls. The skew measures how much fear premium is built into the downside.

Using Skew in Trading

When put skew is very steep (OTM puts much more expensive than calls): Market is fearful. Good time to sell put spreads or buy call spreads.
When skew is flat: Market is complacent. Good time to buy protection cheaply.

CHAPTER 16
Iron Condor Mastery

The Iron Condor is a 4-leg neutral strategy that profits when the underlying stays within a range. It's the professional premium seller's weapon when VIX is elevated.

Structure

Buy 1 OTM Put (lower) + Sell 1 OTM Put (upper) + Sell 1 OTM Call (lower) + Buy 1 OTM Call (upper). Net credit received = max profit. Width of spread minus credit = max loss.

Ideal Conditions for Iron Condor

VIX above 18 (high premium), stock/index in a clear range, no major events in DTE window, sell at 30-delta strikes for optimal risk/reward.

CHAPTER 17
Spreads & Calendar Spreads

Vertical spreads buy and sell same type options at different strikes. They reduce cost and cap both profit and loss — ideal when you have directional view but want defined risk.

Bull Call Spread

Buy ATM Call + Sell OTM Call. Net debit. Profit if underlying rises above upper strike. Cost: difference in premiums. Best when IV is low (buy cheap, sell cheap).

Calendar Spread

Buy far-dated option + Sell near-dated option at same strike. Profits from time decay differential. Near-dated decays faster. Best in low-IV, low-volatility environments.

CHAPTER 18
Expiry Week Playbook

Thursday expiry week is the highest-risk, highest-reward period in NIFTY options. Here is the complete operator playbook:

The Weekly Cycle Pattern

Monday: Stop hunt in both directions. Avoid new positions. Watch only.
Tuesday: Real directional bias emerges post-stop-hunt. Wait for CHoCH signal.
Wednesday: Best risk/reward entry day. Confirmed bias + time decay starting to accelerate on near-dated OTM.
Thursday Expiry: DO NOT hold OTM positions into opening. Exit profitable positions by 2 PM. Gamma acceleration makes moves explosive — both ways.

⚠ Expiry Day Rule

No new entries after 2:00 PM on expiry day. The last 90 minutes have max Gamma — a single large order can move ATM premiums 50–100% in minutes. This is where most retail accounts blow up.
